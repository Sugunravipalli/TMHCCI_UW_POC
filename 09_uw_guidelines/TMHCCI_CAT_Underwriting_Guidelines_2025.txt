1. PURPOSE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
These guidelines govern the acceptance and management of natural catastrophe
exposure within the TMHCCI Commercial Property portfolio. They supplement the
main Underwriting Guidelines and take precedence where CAT-specific provisions
apply.

2. CAT PERILS COVERED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
   - Named Windstorm (Hurricane, Typhoon, Cyclone)
   - Tornado and Severe Convective Storm
   - Earthquake
   - Flood (riverine and coastal)
   - Wildfire
   - Winter Storm (ice, snow load, freeze)

3. WIND / NAMED STORM GUIDELINES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 WIND TIER DEFINITIONS

   Tier 1 - HIGH EXPOSURE
   Counties/parishes within 1 mile of coast in:
   - Florida (all coastal counties)
   - Texas (Galveston, Cameron, Nueces, Aransas, Calhoun, Matagorda,
     Brazoria, Jefferson, Chambers, Harris coastal)
   - Louisiana (Cameron, Vermilion, Iberia, St. Mary, Terrebonne,
     Lafourche, Jefferson, Plaquemines, St. Bernard, Orleans coastal)
   - Mississippi (Harrison, Hancock, Jackson)
   - Alabama (Mobile, Baldwin)
   - Caribbean (all territories)

   Tier 2 - MODERATE EXPOSURE
   Counties/parishes within 1-25 miles of coast in Tier 1 states,
   plus all SC, NC, GA, VA coastal counties.

   Tier 3 - LOW EXPOSURE
   All other areas within 100 miles of Gulf or Atlantic coast.

   Tier 4 - MINIMAL EXPOSURE
   All inland locations > 100 miles from Gulf or Atlantic coast.

3.2 WIND UNDERWRITING LIMITS

   Tier     | Max Gross per Location | Max Net per Location | Deductible Minimum
   ---------|----------------------|---------------------|-------------------
   Tier 1   | $50M                 | $15M                | 5% of location TIV
   Tier 2   | $100M                | $25M                | 3% of location TIV
   Tier 3   | $200M                | $25M                | 2% of location TIV
   Tier 4   | $300M                | $25M                | $50,000

3.3 WIND AGGREGATE LIMITS (100-YEAR RETURN PERIOD)
   - Maximum Gross Aggregate PML:     $200,000,000
   - Maximum Net Aggregate PML:       $75,000,000
   - Florida sub-aggregate limit:     $100M gross / $35M net
   - Texas Gulf sub-aggregate limit:  $60M gross / $20M net
   - Caribbean sub-aggregate limit:   $40M gross / $10M net

3.4 WIND RATE MINIMUMS (per $1,000 TIV, wind portion only)
   Tier 1:    $3.50 minimum
   Tier 2:    $1.50 minimum
   Tier 3:    $0.50 minimum
   Tier 4:    Included in base rate

3.5 CARIBBEAN SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS
   - SVP approval required for all Caribbean risks
   - Facultative reinsurance MANDATORY
   - Maximum net retention: $10M per risk
   - Building must be engineered to withstand Category 3+ hurricane
   - Emergency response plan documentation required
   - Backup power (minimum 72 hours) required

4. EARTHQUAKE GUIDELINES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 SEISMIC ZONE DEFINITIONS

   Zone 5 - HIGHEST EXPOSURE
   Within 5 miles of known active fault in CA, Pacific NW, New Madrid zone.

   Zone 4 - HIGH EXPOSURE
   California (all areas), Pacific Northwest (Seattle, Portland metros),
   Alaska, Hawaii.

   Zone 3 - MODERATE EXPOSURE
   Utah (Wasatch Front), Nevada, Tennessee/Missouri (New Madrid),
   South Carolina (Charleston).

   Zone 2 - LOW EXPOSURE
   All other areas with historical seismic activity.

   Zone 1 - MINIMAL EXPOSURE
   All remaining areas.

4.2 EARTHQUAKE UNDERWRITING LIMITS

   Zone     | Max Gross per Location | Max Net per Location | Deductible Minimum
   ---------|----------------------|---------------------|-------------------
   Zone 5   | $50M                 | $10M                | 5% of location TIV
   Zone 4   | $100M                | $20M                | 3% of location TIV
   Zone 3   | $150M                | $25M                | 2% of location TIV
   Zone 2   | $250M                | $25M                | $100,000
   Zone 1   | $300M                | $25M                | $50,000

4.3 EARTHQUAKE AGGREGATE LIMITS (250-YEAR RETURN PERIOD)
   - Maximum Gross Aggregate PML:     $100,000,000
   - Maximum Net Aggregate PML:       $40,000,000
   - California sub-aggregate:        $60M gross / $25M net
   - Pacific NW sub-aggregate:        $30M gross / $12M net

4.4 EARTHQUAKE CONSTRUCTION REQUIREMENTS
   Zone 4-5 risks:
   - Seismic engineering certification required
   - Buildings must meet current building code seismic standards
   - Pre-1980 unreinforced masonry: DECLINE
   - Soft story construction: maximum $10M per building with retrofit plan

5. FLOOD GUIDELINES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1 FLOOD ZONE DEFINITIONS (per FEMA mapping)
   Zone A / AE / AH / AO:    Special Flood Hazard Area (100-year floodplain)
   Zone V / VE:               Coastal high hazard area (100-year + wave action)
   Zone B / X500:             Moderate flood hazard (500-year floodplain)
   Zone C / X:                Minimal flood hazard

5.2 FLOOD UNDERWRITING LIMITS

   Zone     | Max Sublimit per Location | Deductible Minimum
   ---------|--------------------------|-------------------
   A / AE   | $5,000,000               | $250,000
   V / VE   | $2,500,000               | $500,000 or 2% TIV
   B / X500 | $10,000,000              | $100,000
   C / X    | Full limits              | Per policy deductible

5.3 FLOOD AGGREGATE LIMITS
   - Maximum Gross Flood PML:    $75,000,000
   - Maximum Net Flood PML:      $30,000,000

6. WILDFIRE GUIDELINES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
   - WUI (Wildland-Urban Interface) areas: maximum $25M per location
   - Defensible space > 100 feet required
   - Fire-resistant roofing and exterior materials preferred
   - California WUI: 10% rate surcharge
   - Wildfire protection plan documentation required for TIV > $50M

7. CAT MODELLING REQUIREMENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

7.1 WHEN MODELLING IS REQUIRED
   - Any single location TIV > $50M in Tier 1-2 or Zone 4-5
   - Any portfolio with aggregate TIV > $100M in CAT-exposed areas
   - All Caribbean risks regardless of TIV
   - Any risk triggering aggregate limit review

7.2 ACCEPTED MODELS
   - RMS RiskLink / RMS Risk Intelligence (preferred)
   - AIR Touchstone
   - CoreLogic RQE

7.3 RETURN PERIODS TO ANALYSE
   - 50-year, 100-year, 250-year, 500-year OEP and AEP
   - Results must be provided to CAT management before binding

8. AGGREGATE MONITORING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
   - CAT management provides weekly aggregate position reports
   - Aggregate headroom must be confirmed before binding any risk with
     PML > $5M at 100-year return
   - Aggregate limits are hard caps -- no exceptions without CUO approval
   - Quarter-end aggregate review triggers automatic appetite adjustment
     if limits are > 80% utilised

9. FACULTATIVE REINSURANCE FOR CAT RISKS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
   - Fac MUST be placed before binding when:
     (a) Net exposure exceeds retention limits in this document
     (b) Caribbean territory
     (c) Any single location PML > $15M at 100-year return
   - Fac pricing must be included in rate adequacy calculation
   - Fac slip must specify exact locations and perils covered
   - Minimum fac security: AM Best A- (Excellent) or equivalent
